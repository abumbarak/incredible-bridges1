London Bridge

Information

London Bridge is a suspension bridge that crosses the River Thames in London, England. It is located in the City of London, between the Tower of London and Southwark. The bridge is 1,024 feet (312 m) long and 62 feet (19 m) wide. It has two main towers, each of which is 213 feet (65 m) high.

London Bridge was built in 1831 by John Rennie. It was the first suspension bridge to be built in England, and it was the longest suspension bridge in the world at the time of its completion. The bridge was originally made of stone, but it was rebuilt in steel in 1967.

London Bridge is a popular tourist destination. It is one of the most photographed bridges in the world. The bridge is also featured in many songs and poems.
